Grammar Cheat Sheet

Explicit grammar rules, patterns, and formality levels you'll encounter in the lessons.

Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) Word Order

Korean sentences follow a different word order than English. The verb comes at the end.

Pattern: Subject + Object + Verb

Korean: 저는 밥을 먹어요

Literal: I rice eat

English: I eat rice.

Formality Levels

Korean has different speech levels based on context and relationship. Using the correct level is essential for respect and cultural appropriateness.

🎩 Formal / Polite (존댓말 - Jondaetmal)

Use with: Strangers, elders, authority figures, in formal situations

안녕하세요.

Hello (formal)

😊 Informal / Casual (반말 - Banmal)

Use with: Close friends, peers, children

안녕!

Hello (informal)

Particles (조사 - Josa)

Particles attach to nouns to show their grammatical function.

은/는 (Topic Marker)

Marks the topic of a sentence

저는 학생이에요

As for me, (I) am a student

을/를 (Object Marker)

Marks the direct object of a verb

밥을 먹어요

Eat rice

에 (Location/Direction)

Indicates where or to where

서울에 가요

Go to Seoul

Participles & Tenses

Korean verbs don't conjugate like English, but add endings to show tense and mood.

Present: -어요/-아요

먹어요

eat (present)

Past: -었어요/-았어요

먹었어요

ate (past)

Future: -(으)ㄹ 거예요

먹을 거예요

will eat
